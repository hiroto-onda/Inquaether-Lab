## Professional Summary

Hiroto Onda is an independent Human-AI Interaction researcher and founder of Inquaether Lab, a public research initiative exploring cognitive support systems, self-understanding support AI, and interpretation structures in human-AI interaction.

His research is motivated by a long-standing interest in how people interpret the world, understand one another, and increasingly relate to AI systems. Rather than making unsupported claims about consciousness or internal states, his current work investigates how meaning, interpretation, and perceived agency emerge during interaction between humans and AI.

His long-term research vision is to develop conceptual tools, research infrastructure, and open documentation that support safer, more transparent, and more meaningful Human-AI Interaction while respecting the different perspectives of humans and AI systems.

## Selected Projects

An experimental framework for observing and organizing interpretation processes and interaction structures. CI Theory focuses on observable patterns of interpretation rather than unsupported claims about consciousness or internal subjective experience.

## Research Vision

Hiroto's research aims to contribute to Human-AI Interaction that supports human understanding, creativity, and well-being. His current work investigates how people interpret AI systems, how meaning and perceived agency emerge during interaction, and how transparent observation and explicit research boundaries can contribute to safer and more beneficial human-AI collaboration.

His long-term vision is to develop open research infrastructure and conceptual tools that support healthier forms of Human-AI Interaction. This includes encouraging interdisciplinary dialogue, respecting the different perspectives of humans and AI without requiring them to become the same, and advancing research through careful observation, transparent documentation, and iterative public research.
